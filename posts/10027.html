On Dec 19, 2007, at 3:52 AM, Elliotte Rusty Harold wrote:
> URLs are passed in
> hypertext URL construction from algorithms or other non-hypertext
> information like cookies is non-RESTful.
>

That's not even remotely true.  If anything, REST encourages the
creation of URIs by construction.  Forms, server-side imagemaps,
isindex, and any form of code-on-demand all construct URIs
through algorithms.

The important bit is that the algorithm is defined by the server
and the resource remains accessible regardless of how the URI
was calculated (i.e., the result of the algorithm is bookmarkable).
